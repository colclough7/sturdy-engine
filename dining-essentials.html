Dining Essentials

Everything you need to know before you sit down to eat in Taniti — payment, tipping, connectivity, and holiday hours.

Currency & Tipping

Currency & Payment

Taniti uses the US Dollar (USD). Most sit-down restaurants and the resort accept Visa and Mastercard. Smaller local eateries, the harbour night market stalls, and street food vendors are often cash only — it is always worth carrying small bills when eating out around Taniti City.

Official currency: US Dollar (USD)

Tipping Customs

Tipping is not a strong local tradition on Taniti, but it is warmly appreciated at sit-down restaurants. A tip of 10% is considered generous at a local restaurant. At the resort's Coral Table, 15% is more customary. You do not need to tip at food stalls, the night market, or when buying takeaway.

Power & Connectivity

Power Sockets

Taniti uses US-style Type A and B sockets. If you are travelling from outside North America you will need a plug adaptor. Most restaurants in Taniti City and at the resort have USB charging points available for customers.

110v / 60 Hz · Type A & B sockets

Wi-Fi & Data

Free Wi-Fi is available at the resort, most hotels, and the majority of sit-down restaurants in Taniti City. The harbour area and the night market have a public Wi-Fi hotspot. Mobile data coverage is good across Taniti City and the coastal road, but patchy in the rainforest interior and highlands.
