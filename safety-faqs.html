Safety & FAQs

Taniti is a safe and welcoming island. Here is everything you need to know to travel with confidence.

Home › Plan › Safety & FAQs

Safety Information

General Safety

Taniti is considered a very safe destination for tourists. Petty crime is rare and violent crime is extremely uncommon. As with any destination, keep an eye on your belongings at the night market and in crowded areas, and avoid unlit beaches after midnight.

Emergency Contacts

Taniti has a small but well-equipped hospital in Taniti City and a medical clinic near Merriton Landing. In an emergency, dial 112 from any phone on the island. Our FakeRoads team is also available 24 hours on +1 (808) 555-0199 for guest assistance.

Health & Sun Safety

The tropical sun on Taniti is strong year-round. Wear high-SPF sunscreen, a hat, and stay hydrated, especially on hiking tours. Tap water is safe to drink across the island. No vaccinations are currently required for entry, though travel insurance is strongly recommended.

Travel Insurance

We strongly recommend purchasing comprehensive travel insurance before your trip. This should cover medical treatment, emergency evacuation, trip cancellation, and lost luggage. Cover for water sports and hiking activities is worth checking if you plan to join any of our tours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Taniti safe for solo travellers? +
Yes — Taniti is widely regarded as one of the safer Pacific Island destinations for solo travellers, including solo women. The local community is welcoming and used to tourists. Standard common-sense precautions apply, such as not leaving valuables unattended on the beach and staying aware of your surroundings at night.
What is the best time of year to visit Taniti? +
The dry season from July to September is the most popular time to visit — clear skies, calm seas, and ideal conditions for snorkelling, hiking, and sailing. The shoulder seasons (April–June and October–November) are also excellent and tend to be quieter and slightly cheaper. The wet season (January–March) brings frequent afternoon showers but the island remains warm and very swimmable.
Do I need a visa to visit Taniti? +
Most visitors from the US, EU, UK, Australia, and New Zealand do not require a visa for stays of up to 30 days. A valid passport is required for all visitors. Always check with your country's foreign travel advisory for the most current entry requirements before booking your trip.
Can I use my credit card on the island? +
Yes — most hotels, restaurants, and tour operators accept Visa and Mastercard. American Express is accepted at the resort and a handful of larger restaurants. We recommend carrying some US Dollar cash for the harbour night market, small local food stalls, and taxis, as these are often cash only.
What should I pack for a trip to Taniti? +
Light, breathable clothing is ideal year-round. Essential items include high-SPF sunscreen, insect repellent, a reusable water bottle, comfortable walking shoes, and reef-safe sunscreen if you plan to snorkel. A light rain jacket is worth packing during the wet and transitional seasons. For the volcano sunrise trek, bring a warm layer as the summit can be cool before dawn.
How do I get from the airport to my accommodation? +
The public bus runs from Taniti Airport to Taniti City for $2 and operates from early morning until around 10pm. Taxis are also available outside the terminal and a ride to Taniti City typically costs under $15. If you are staying at the resort, they offer a complimentary shuttle — confirm this when you book. FakeRoads guests can also arrange an airport transfer through us in advance.
