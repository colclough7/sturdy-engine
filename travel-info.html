Need To Know

Currency

Taniti uses the US Dollar (USD) as its official currency. Most hotels, restaurants, and tour operators accept major credit cards, but it is worth carrying some cash for the night market, small food stalls, and local taxis.

Official currency: US Dollar (USD)

Power & Voltage

Taniti uses the same power sockets and voltage as the United States. If you are travelling from Europe, Australia, or the UK you will need a plug adaptor. Most hotels have universal sockets in the bathrooms for shavers and small devices.

110v / 60 Hz · Type A & B sockets

Public Holidays

Most businesses and restaurants remain open on public holidays, though government offices and some local shops will be closed. The harbour night market runs as normal on holidays and is often livelier than usual.

New Year's Day · Island Day (Mar 15) · Harvest Festival (Aug) · Christmas Day

Visa & Entry

Most visitors from the US, EU, UK, Australia, and New Zealand do not require a visa for stays of up to 30 days. A valid passport is required. Check with your country's foreign travel advisory for the most current entry requirements before booking.
